# J3 — Owner reads "which dishes lost money this week" (Sunday night)

The Owner-side mobile dashboard. The visual surface is [mock-j3-owner-mobile.html](variants/mock-j3-owner-mobile.html); this doc anchors that mock to the FR catalogue and to the persona's actual reading conditions (phone, dim room, one hand).

## Goal

Roberto answers two questions in 30 seconds:

1. *Which dishes are losing me margin this week?* (bottom 5)
2. *Which ones are saving the average?* (top 5)

The view is read-only. He does not edit recipes — that is Lourdes' job. If a dish is bleeding, he WhatsApps her and she fixes it on the kitchen tablet (J1 / J2).

## Trigger

Sunday 22:30. Dinner finished, two restaurants closed for the week. Roberto is on the sofa with the phone in his right hand. He opens openTrattOS via the home-screen icon. He lands directly on **Margin** — the default tab for the Owner role.

## Walkthrough

The numbers below correspond to the regions of [mock-j3-owner-mobile.html](variants/mock-j3-owner-mobile.html).

1. **Open the app.** The Owner-role default route is the Margin tab. No login splash, no marketing card. Sticky header shows `Palafito · Combined` (location filter chip) and `Last 7 days` (time-window chip). Page title `Margin watch · 28 Apr 2026 · Sunday` in serif so the eye lands on what surface this is. (DESIGN.md §3 — serif on display "menu nouns" only.)
2. **Read the bottom 5 first.** Section header `Bottom 5 — losing margin`. Five `MenuItemRanker` rows, ranked 1–5 by distance below target (worst first). The eye lands on the margin number in `--destructive` paprika because that is the cue Roberto trained himself on over 20 years of running restaurants. He sees `Risotto de hongos · 56.8 % · -13.2 pp from 70 % target · 12 sold last 7d`. FR33, FR38.
3. **Skim the next four.** *Carbonara* 58.0 %, *Lasaña* 61.5 % both flag paprika (sub-target). *Tagliatelle bolognesa* 77.1 % and *Pizza margarita* 75.4 % render in `--ink` because they are still above target despite being in the bottom-5-by-trend. The colour cue is "below target", not "ranked low" — Roberto's eye filters out the still-healthy ones in one beat.
4. **Read the top 5.** Section header `Top 5 — keeping margin`. Same row layout, margins in `--success` sage. *Tiramisú* 85.9 %, *Tagliatelle al pesto* 82.2 %, *Spaghetti aglio e olio* 81.2 %, *Bruschetta clásica* 80.0 %, *Insalata caprese* 78.2 %. The ones with `+` deltas confirm the trend; the `Insalata caprese -0.4 pp` one is a quiet warning. FR33, FR38.
5. **Drill into the bleeder.** Roberto taps the *Risotto de hongos* row. The recipe view opens read-only, with a `CostDeltaTable` showing per-component deltas vs. the previous 7-day window. He sees the *boletus* line jumped 22 % on the current supplier batch. (Same drill-down spine as J2, but with no edit affordances on the Owner path — see Decisions below.) FR39.
6. **Decide and exit.** He WhatsApps Lourdes: *"baja el risotto del menú hasta que vuelva el precio del boletus"*. Closes the app. **Total time: under 60 s, under 3 taps**, exactly as the PRD's J3 narrative says.

`MenuItemRanker` (mobile-first stack rows, DESIGN.md §4) · `MarginPanel` (compact form, used inside the drill-down) · `CostDeltaTable` (read-only on this path, DESIGN.md §4) · sticky bottom tab-bar (mobile pattern, not a named DESIGN.md component but governed by §5 Spacing — 48 px touch targets — and §7 Guidelines).

## Edge cases (covered elsewhere or noted)

- **Single-location operator.** When the org has only one Location, the location chip renders as the location name without a filter pill behind it. No `Combined` toggle. Prevents a meaningless control.
- **Empty dashboard** (org just signed up, no MenuItems). The page renders a single empty-state card: `No MenuItems yet. Margin watch lights up after your first published recipe.` No skeleton-flash; no "Get started" CTA — that ceremony belongs in onboarding, not here.
- **All margins above target.** The bottom-5 section still renders; numbers all flag `--ink`, not `--destructive`. The header copy stays `Bottom 5 — losing margin` because it is descriptive of rank, not of state. Roberto reads the deltas to spot trends even when nothing is below target.
- **Time-window with sparse data** (fewer than 5 sold). Rows still render in rank order; the volume sub-line displays `< 5 sold last 7d` in `--mute`. Roberto knows to discount low-volume noise.
- **Drill-down for an Owner without supplier visibility.** The `CostDeltaTable` shows component-level deltas. M3 will add SupplierItem batch attribution; in M2 the read-only path stops at "this ingredient went up X %".

## Decisions specific to J3

- **Mobile-first, not a responsive afterthought.** The Owner persona reads on phone Sunday night. DESIGN.md §8 explicitly device-locks Owner to mobile for read-only dashboards. The tablet kitchen surface (J1 / J2) is irrelevant here — he is on a sofa, not in a kitchen. Designing this view tablet-first and shrinking would have produced a card grid that wastes the tall phone canvas.
- **Bottom 5 first, top 5 second.** Loss attention beats gain attention for this persona. Roberto's job at 22:30 is to *spot the bleed*, not to celebrate. The PRD narrative confirms: "*Bottom 5 highlights Tartar de atún…*". Putting top-5 first would force him to scroll past the good news to find the bad — a tax on the 30-second budget.
- **Margin pp delta vs target is the headline number, not absolute profit.** Roberto thinks in margin %. He has been running restaurants for 20 years; the language of his trade is "this dish does 70". Showing absolute profit (e.g. `€129 lost on risotto this week`) would be technically correct and entirely wrong as a primary cue — it forces translation. Margin % maps directly onto his mental model.
- **Drill-down is read-only on the Owner path.** RBAC has him as `OWNER` with read access to recipes (per personas-jtbd.md §2 RBAC matrix and PRD-M2 FR46). He could legally edit; he doesn't, by design. The UI suppresses every edit affordance on this path. If something needs to change, he WhatsApps Lourdes — that is the actual workflow at Grupo Palafito and the dashboard should not pretend otherwise. (Avoids a multi-role UI that confuses Owner-with-edit-button vs Manager-who-actually-edits.)
- **Time-window toggle in the header.** Default 7 d; options 7 d / 30 d / 90 d / custom. The chip in the sticky header reads `Last 7 days`; tapping opens a sheet from the bottom (thumb-reachable) with the four options. Custom opens a date-range picker. The default is 7 d because the PRD's J3 narrative is explicitly the Sunday-night recap; longer windows are for end-of-month review.
- **Multi-location chip.** Default `Combined` (all locations the Owner has access to, summed). Tap → bottom sheet listing each Location plus `Combined`. Roberto operates two restaurants (`Palafito Centro` + `Palafito Playa`); the chip carries both names + `Combined` as the third option. Single-location operators see the location name as a static label, no chip behaviour. (See Edge cases.)
- **No emoji on rank numbers.** DESIGN.md §1 forbids emoji icons; rank circles render literal `1` `2` `3` `4` `5`. The shape (`--surface` filled circle with `--ink` numeral) carries the role.
- **Bottom tab bar is the navigation, not a side menu.** Phone portrait wants thumb-reach. Tabs `Margin · Recipes · Reports · Settings` sit in the bottom 1/3 of the screen, each ≥ 48 px. The Owner lands on `Margin` by default; `Recipes` is read-only browse; `Reports` is the longer-window P&L view (post-MVP placeholder); `Settings` is org-level (target margin, locations, etc., per FR37).

## Notes for implementation

- **Sticky header + sticky bottom tab bar.** Both are `position: sticky` (header) / `position: fixed` (tab bar). The scrollable region between them is the rank list. The tab bar uses `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` so it sits above the iOS home indicator without overlap.
- **Touch targets.** Every row is ≥ 64 px tall (comfortably above the 48 px floor); the rank circle alone is 36 px but the row hit area extends edge-to-edge. The chevron at the right is decorative — the whole row is tappable. Tab bar items are 56 px tall × full-width-divided.
- **Tabular numerics.** Every digit on the page (margin %, sold count, the bottom aggregate `73.8 %`) carries `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums lining-nums`. When the time window changes from 7 d to 30 d, the numbers re-render without dancing.
- **Colour as cue, never alone.** `--destructive` paprika on sub-target margin is the *cue Roberto's eye lands on first*; the textual delta line (`-13.2 pp from 70 % target`) carries the same information for screen readers and for users with red-green deficiency. WCAG-AA verified on `--destructive` on `--bg` (~5.6:1) and on `--success` on `--bg` (~4.6:1).
- **`prefers-reduced-motion`.** The drill-down panel slides in at 300 ms by default; under reduced-motion it appears instantly. No celebration anywhere — Roberto did not log in to be celebrated, he logged in to know what is bleeding.
- **First-paint budget.** Owner dashboard load < 1.5 s p95 (PRD NFR). The page is server-rendered HTML with the rank list inlined; no client-side data-fetch waterfall on the first view. The system-font stack and `font-display: swap` on Fraunces (DESIGN.md §3) hold the budget on a slow 4G connection from a sofa Wi-Fi.
- **Reading-mode brightness.** The Pulcinella palette is already low-glare on warm cream. No additional dark-mode is required for M2; the page reads comfortably at 30 % phone brightness in a dim room. Dark-mode is M2.x.
